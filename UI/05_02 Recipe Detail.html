Apple Pie

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour 1 cup (2 sticks) 1 tsp salt 1 tbsp sugar 6–8 tbsp ice water 6–7 medium apples ¾ cup granulated sugar ¼ cup brown sugar 2 tbsp all-purpose flour 1 tsp cinnamon ¼ tsp nutmeg 1 tbsp lemon juice 1 tbsp butter (for dotting) 1 egg (for egg wash)

Step 1: Make the dough In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Cut in butter with a pastry cutter or fork until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing just until dough holds together. Divide dough into two disks, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.  Step 2: Prepare the filling In a large bowl, toss sliced apples with sugars, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Let sit for about 10 minutes so the apples release some juices.  Step 3: Assemble the pie Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disk of dough and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Fill with apple mixture, piling slightly in the center. Dot with small pieces of butter. Roll out the second disk of dough and place over filling. Trim excess, crimp edges, and cut slits in the top for steam vents (or make a lattice top).  Step 4: Bake Brush the top with beaten egg (for shine). Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 35–45 minutes more, until crust is golden brown and filling is bubbling. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing so the filling sets.
